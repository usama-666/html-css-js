Quaid - e - Azam

1876-1948

Founder of Pakistan

Muhammad Ali Jinnah often referred to as Quaid-e-Azam (Great Leader), was a towering figure in South Asian history whose leadership and vision shaped the course of the Indian subcontinent. Born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, then part of British India, Jinnah emerged as a prominent lawyer and politician during India's struggle for independence. His early career as a successful barrister in Bombay showcased his exceptional legal acumen and oratory skills, earning him widespread respect. Jinnah's political journey saw him initially aligned with the Indian National Congress, advocating for Hindu-Muslim unity and constitutional reforms. However, disillusionment with Congress' policies led him to champion Muslim rights and ultimately become the leader of the All India Muslim League, where he articulated the Two-Nation Theory, advocating for a separate Muslim-majority nation, which culminated in the creation of Pakistan in 1947. Jinnah's leadership as Pakistan's first Governor-General laid the foundation for a democratic and inclusive state, guided by the principles of unity, faith, and discipline, leaving an indelible mark on Pakistan's history and identity
